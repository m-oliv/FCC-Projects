Joe MacMillan

The Visionary

Joe MacMillan is a charismatic ex-IBM sales executive. After he quits IBM, he moves to Dallas and shows up at Cardiff Electric's headquarters. He talks his way into a senior position at the company. Joe creates a team of engineers with the sole purpose of reverse-engineering IBM's computer and creating a better version of the product. Joe handles the marketing and sales of this project, sometimes almost going overboard while doing so. To handle the hardware, Joe challenges Gordon Clark, a good engineer who's down on his luck. The software is handled by Cameron Howe, a young programmer, with whom Joe develops a volatile relationship.

Building Cardiff's computer tests Joe's relationships, getting him friends and a lot of enemies. When the project goes horribly wrong, Joe almost gives up on it. However, Gordon, with the help of his wife, and Cameron manage to save the day. Together, Joe and Co. present the prototype of their computer - nicknamed The Giant - at Comdex. Even though the Giant is a sales success, Joe is not pleased to find out that their project is surpassed by Apple's Macintosh. He ends up burning a shipment of computers and leaving the company and his team behind. When Joe is seen again, he has a totally different life and is still searching for new and exciting tech projects.

“Computers aren’t the thing. They’re the thing that gets us to the thing.”
